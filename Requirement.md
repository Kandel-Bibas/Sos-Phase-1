Here are a few examples of questions we currently would ask to my staff or our partners when reviewing regs and state law by hand. Nowhere near an exhaustive list, but a few prime examples. I hope this is helpful. Happy to follow-up and elaborate more if needed. 

How do the fines (or fees) outlined in XYZ state entity's rules and regulations compare to Mississippi law? 
In this situation we are checking for statutory authorization of fees or fines. We often see fines/fees of $X more than statute even allows for said fine or fee to be. Sometimes the state law is silent and leaves the full authority to the state regulating entity to determine and there is no min or max in state law, other times state law may set a cap of $50 but the board is charging more etc. 
 How many times does the term XYZ appear in state entity's rules and regulations and where does term XYZ appear in state law in reference to said state entity (need link or references to each appearance) 
For example, how many times does the term "prohibit" appear in the board of medical licensure's rules and regs and how many times does it appear in state laws relevant to their board. 
If someone moved to Mississippi today and held a XYZ license in another state, could they obtain licensure reciprocity here in Mississippi, or would they have to start the licensure process over again? 
What is the licensure renewal fee for XYZ license issued by XYZ state entity? 
How does said licensure fee in Mississippi compare to the same licensure renewal fee in XYZ states? 
When was the last time XYZ rule (or statute) was amended or adopted? 
What is the statutory authority for XYZ entity to adopt XYZ regulation? 
We often find boards/agencies adopting rules and regulations that they are not legally authorized to enforce. 
Mississippi's board of medical licensure has a temporary license for XYZ license category, do other states do the same thing? If so, is ours easier or more complex to obtain? 
How do XYZ testing requirements for licensure in Mississippi compare to XYZ state.
